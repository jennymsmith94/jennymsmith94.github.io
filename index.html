Dissertation Research

The hippocampus is a region of the brain critical for the formation and storage of new spatial and episodic memories. I analyzed a previously recorded in vivo electrophysiology dataset containing continuous local field potential (LFP) signal and single neuron activity to identify changes in hippocampal network activity over the course of a learning experience in rats.
I found that a specific sub-group of hippocampal neurons in area CA1, which is the main output region of the hippocampus, shifts their activity within sharp wave - ripple events (SWRs) during a learning experience, and this shift persists into the post- experience rest period. SWRs are high frequency (150-250 Hz) LFP events associated with the reactivation of experience-related neural activity and are thought to underlie memory processes such as consolidation and retrieval.
This sub-group of CA1 neurons are located in the superficial sub-layer of CA1, which is more highly excited by CA3, a hippocampal sub-region directly upstream of CA1 that is considered to be the primary short-term memory storage site in the hippocampus, and the origin site for SWR events. Superficial neurons exhibited a ~180 degree shift in phase locking to the underlying slow gamma oscillation (25-55 Hz) during SWRs. This phase locking shift was associated with the synchronization of deep and superficial populations within the slow gamma oscillation during SWRS.
Slow gamma in CA1 is driven by CA3 input, and SWRs are generated in CA3. This oscillation has also been shown to entrain the content of replay in CA1. In line with this evidence, I hypothesize that the synchronization of deep and superficial CA1 populations leads to more efficient communication of hippocampal replay content to downstream brain regions such as the subiculum or retrosplenial cortex. This would facilitate memory processes by driving more robust synaptic reorganization in cortical circuits, or by increasing the signal to noise ratio of replay content to allow for more detailed information to be used in downstream decision making steps.

Google Data Analytics Certificate Capstone

For this project, I identified key differences in usage of a bikeshare service between two user groups: annual members and casual riders. In this scenario, I was asked to deliver 3 recommendations to guide a future marketing strategy aimed at converting casual riders to annual members.

Skills:

  • Used R to merge, clean, and analyze 12 months of ride data (over 5.1 million rides)
